The doctoral dissertation titled "Brand Management Model for Establishing Long-Term Customer Relationships" introduces relevant models for effective brand and customer portfolio management, including the potential for integrating brand management and long-term customer relationship management. The thesis begins with a chapter overview, briefly outlining each section's content. The second chapter discusses the concept and history of branding, focusing on brand architecture and brand knowledge, and delves into the brand management process, particularly the brand resonance model. It also explains terms like brand value, added value, and brand financial value. The third chapter focuses on relationship management, defining it and discussing CRM database and the Payne model of customer relationship management. It also explores customer relationship management models, segmentation, sub-segmentation, and categorization in CRM. The fourth chapter examines the relationship management between a company and its customers and the models of these relationships. The fifth chapter presents a specific brand management model for establishing long-term customer relationships, focusing on a branding model based on customer perspective and Suzanne Fernie's brand and customer relationship model. It also discusses concepts like loyalty and customer satisfaction. The sixth chapter identifies points of integration between the brand management model and long-term customer relationship management, providing a detailed analysis and comparison of both concepts. It emphasizes the segmentation process and customer categorization, introducing the new concept of "relationship branding". The chapter concludes with the presentation of an integral model. The chapter's final section presents research findings and hypothesis tests, offering final thoughts on the thesis's scientific contribution and its implications for managers, as well as potential future research directions. The paper concludes with a list of references and contributions to the dissertation.
