'This thesis scrutinises the B2B operations within the electronic media industry. It delves deeply into the analysis of electronic media services, along with the shifts brought about by contemporary e-business technologies in the utilisation of these services. The focus is on customer relationship management as a crucial element of electronic media's B2B operations. Particular emphasis is given to progressive strategies and technologies for managing relationships with stakeholders in the electronic media sector. Moreover, the thesis explores ways to enhance relationship management between public broadcasters and stakeholders through the adoption of cutting-edge e-business technologies like social media, digital and interactive TV, and mobile computing. The thesis also creates a customer relationship model for the B2B operations of electronic media. This model facilitates the application of advanced CRM concepts and services and fosters collaboration with stakeholders. It also discusses opportunities to merge various media types and services into a single electronic media business system. This amalgamation pertains to information, processes, application components, and human resources. In the practical segment of the thesis, the newly developed customer relationship model for B2B operations was assessed. This assessment took place within the e-business system of Radio Television of Serbia. The experimental findings uphold the overarching hypothesis that the incorporation and amalgamation of CRM into the business system of electronic media bolsters business performance, harmonises all business processes of electronic media, and results in increased client satisfaction and loyalty.
