The doctoral thesis titled "Brand Management Model for Establishing Long-Term Customer Relationships" presents relevant models that businesses employ for effective brand and customer portfolio management. It also explores the potential for integrating brand management and long-term customer relationship management. The thesis begins with a chapter overview, briefly summarizing each section's content. The second chapter delves into the concept and history of branding, focusing on brand architecture and brand knowledge. It also discusses the brand management process, emphasizing the brand resonance model, and explains terms related to brand value, added value, and brand financial value. The third chapter is dedicated to relationship management, defining it and discussing the CRM database and the Payne model of customer relationship management. It also covers models of customer relationship management, segmentation, sub-segmentation, and categorization in CRM. The fourth chapter concentrates on the management of relationships between a company and its customers, presenting models of such relationships. The fifth chapter develops a specific brand management model aimed at establishing long-term customer relationships, focusing on a branding model from the customer's perspective and the Suzanne Fernie model of brand and customer relationships. It discusses concepts like loyalty and customer satisfaction. The sixth chapter identifies points of integration between the brand management model and long-term customer relationship management, providing a detailed analysis and comparison of both concepts. It emphasizes the segmentation process and customer categorization, introducing the novel concept of "relationship branding". The chapter concludes with the presentation of an integral model and the results of research and hypothesis testing. It also offers final thoughts on the thesis's contribution to the field and its implications for managers, as well as potential directions for future research. The thesis concludes with a list of references and a summary of the dissertation's contributions.
