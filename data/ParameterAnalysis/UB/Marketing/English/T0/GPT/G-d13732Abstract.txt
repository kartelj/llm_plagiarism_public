This thesis examines the B2B aspect of electronic media business, delving into the intricacies of electronic media services and the transformations brought about by contemporary e-business technologies. It underscores the significance of customer relationship management (CRM) in the B2B sector of electronic media. The study pays particular attention to advanced methods and technologies for managing relationships with electronic media stakeholders. It also explores how innovative e-business technologies, such as social media, digital and interactive TV, and mobile computing, can enhance relationship management between public broadcasters and their stakeholders. A CRM model for the B2B electronic media business was developed as part of this thesis, facilitating the application of advanced CRM concepts and services, and fostering collaboration with stakeholders. The study also discusses the potential for integrating various media types and services into a unified electronic media business system, encompassing information, processes, application components, and human resources. The thesis includes an experimental evaluation of the developed CRM model for the B2B business, conducted within the e-business system of the Radio Television of Serbia. The results support the overarching hypothesis that incorporating and integrating CRM into the electronic media business system boosts business performance, synchronises all business processes, and increases client satisfaction and loyalty.
