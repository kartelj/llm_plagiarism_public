The tourism market is becoming increasingly competitive. Tourist destinations that offer superior services and value are becoming the preferred choices for tourists. The activities carried out to provide services to tourists in spas form the value chain of the spa destination. The entities carrying out these activities are the links in the value chain, providing a comprehensive spa product through a series of actions and activities. Tourism business entities strive to offer superior activities to produce a higher quality product. The collective effort of all business entities at the spa destination provides tourists with a holistic spa product composed of various partial products. An analysis and comparison of spa destinations in renowned European countries and Serbia highlight the position, role, and significance of Serbian spas. The comparative analysis uncovers the main strengths and weaknesses in Serbia's spa value chain. The competitive edge of Serbian spas lies in their exceptional natural potential, which could be better leveraged for tourism through a systematic approach.
