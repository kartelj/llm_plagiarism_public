The effect of foreign direct investment on the present account is multifaceted, it could be either beneficial or detrimental depending on the overall amount of investment and its sectoral allocation. The dissertation presented a theoretical and empirical examination of how FDI influences the trade balance and income account of Serbia, particularly focusing on its impact on the Serbian manufacturing industry. Additionally, an evaluation of how foreign direct investment influences technology transfer in Serbia was conducted, along with an investigation into its ability to facilitate access to global markets, its effect on the structure and specialization of exports and imports, and its influence on the appreciation of the exchange rate. The thesis also presented a research into the reasons behind the current account deficit in Central and Eastern European nations, and an examination of how foreign direct investment affects the sustainability of the current account deficit, considering that foreign direct investment is a significant funding source for the current account deficit and can set the stage for financial contagion through various channels.
