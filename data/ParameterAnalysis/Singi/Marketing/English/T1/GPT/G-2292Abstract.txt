The dissertation explores theoretical and hands-on examination of large public corporations in Serbia, with a specific emphasis on their effectiveness, critical evaluation of privatisations in Serbia and other transition nations, proposed models for privatisation, and directive suggestions for privatisation strategies. The study outlines the primary function of public enterprises, compares their efficiency against private enterprises, scrutinises their corporate governance, and offers a thorough global examination of public enterprises and their privatisation. It also provides a summary of the application of contemporary management techniques and their impact on efficiency improvement. Special weightage is given to the significance of public-private collaboration and the need for public enterprises to adopt a corporate structure. The vital role of privatisation in enhancing efficiency and performance of public enterprises is shown. The dissertation also illustrates different views regarding the privatisation and corporatisation of public enterprises such as "Elektroprivreda" and the joint-stock company "Telekom".
