The tourism market is becoming increasingly competitive. Tourist spots with superior offerings, and therefore value, are emerging as competitive choices for tourists. All efforts made to cater to tourists in spas constitute the value chain of the spa destination. The entities carrying out these activities form the links in the value chain, delivering a complete spa experience through a series of actions and activities. Businesses involved in providing tourist services strive for superior activities to deliver a higher quality sub-product. The collective presence of all businesses at the spa destination provides tourists with a comprehensive spa product composed of numerous sub-products. By analyzing spa destinations in renowned European countries and comparing them with Serbian spa destinations, the position, function, and significance of Serbian spas are highlighted. The comparative analysis uncovers the primary strengths and weaknesses in Serbia's spa value chain. The competitive edge of Serbian spas lies in their extraordinary natural potential, which could be better leveraged for tourism through a systematic approach.
