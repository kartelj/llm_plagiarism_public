The dissertation explores the theoretical and empirical examination of large public enterprises in the Republic of Serbia, focusing specifically on their performance efficiency and a critical review of privatizations in Serbia and transition countries. It proposes privatization models and strategies. The paper defines the primary role of public enterprises, compares the efficiency of private and state ownership in these enterprises, and scrutinizes their corporate governance. It also offers a global perspective on the significance of public enterprises and their privatization. The application of modern management tools and their impact on efficiency gains are discussed, with special emphasis on the importance of public-private partnerships and the need for corporatization of public enterprises. The dissertation demonstrates the role of privatization in enhancing efficiency and performance of public enterprises. It also presents viewpoints on the privatizations and corporatization of the public enterprise "Elektroprivreda" and the shareholding company "Telekom".
