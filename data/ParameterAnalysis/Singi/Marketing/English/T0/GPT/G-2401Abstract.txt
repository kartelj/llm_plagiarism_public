Services play a crucial role in the global business landscape, as they are integral to nearly all economic activities and are a significant driving force of the global economy. The need for swift adaptation and continuous quality enhancement aligns with the evolution of the service sector, necessitating a practical, logical, and socio-economically sound development strategy. This paper introduces the concept of customer relationship management (CRM), with a particular focus on its impact on the quality of banking services in Serbia. It discusses current understanding of customer relationship management in banking institutions and how modern business changes influence the CRM system. Given their significance, service quality can be ranked among the most vital tools of service marketing. The paper includes empirical research conducted among Serbian banking service users. The primary aim of the study was to evaluate how CRM affects the quality of banking services in Serbia, customer satisfaction, and consequently, the profitability of the banks within the research's spatial and temporal parameters.
