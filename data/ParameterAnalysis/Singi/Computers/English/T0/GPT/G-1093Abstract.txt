Customer Relationship Management (CRM) is a fundamental principle in modern retail businesses. It's a crucial component for the survival of retail companies in today's competitive business landscape. CRM in retail involves establishing and nurturing long-lasting partnerships with customers, aiming to generate lifelong value for both the customers and the retail companies. What sets CRM apart from other relationship management strategies is its ability to foster unique relationships between retail companies and their customers. 

The management of customer relationship changes relies heavily on accurate information and understanding of customer needs, demands, and behaviors. It also hinges on the development of various communication methods with customers, including verbal and non-verbal, personal and electronic. Managing changes in customer relationships involves altering the behavior of organizations towards their customers and vice versa. 

Given the unpredictable and time-sensitive nature of business relationships between retailers and customers, it's crucial for modern retailers to adopt a Planned Approach to manage customer relationships in the long term. High-quality services, informed by comprehensive research into customer needs, stimulate customer interest in the activities of retail companies. This ultimately leads to increased customer satisfaction, trust, and loyalty. 

The research conducted for this thesis has helped identify key factors in building relationships with customers, fostering their trust, and securing their loyalty. It has also shed light on the elements of change and how to manage these changes in customer relationships within the Serbian retail industry.
